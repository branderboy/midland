Smart Chat & Booking System

How it works

Your website chat turns conversations into booked, tracked jobs.

There is no contact form to fill out. A visitor just talks to the chat like a text message, and the system quietly captures the lead, books the visit, and logs everything in your CRM — hands-free, 24/7.

1

A visitor starts chatting — no form required

The assistant greets visitors and answers questions about your services 24/7, using your real site content. It reads like a person texting back, not a robot or a brochure.

Free-hand conversation
2

It captures their name and email — inside the conversation

When someone wants to schedule or get a quote, the assistant asks for their name and best email in one friendly line and pulls it straight out of what they typed. The chat is the form — the lead is captured the moment they share it.

Lead captured automatically
3

Then it offers a time on your calendar

Only after the name and email are captured does a “Pick a time” button appear, sending the visitor to your Calendly. The link is tagged behind the scenes so the booking always ties back to the right person.

Calendly booking
4

The booking flows straight into your CRM

The moment they book, the contact is created or updated, tagged as booked, the deal moves to the Booked stage, and the job is created in ServiceM8 — automatically. You get an email, and the lead shows up in your pipeline. Cancellations reverse it.

Contact · tag · deal · job
5

After the job, a review request goes out

When the job is marked complete, the system follows up automatically to ask for a review — so every finished job has a chance to become a five-star rating.

Automatic review request

What you get out of it

  • Every lead is captured — even at 2am, with no form for the customer to abandon.
  • Every booking is tracked — booked visits land in your CRM and pipeline, tagged and attributed.
  • Nothing falls through the cracks — chat, Calendly, and CRM all talk to each other automatically.
  • Less manual work — no copying names into spreadsheets, no chasing no-shows by hand.
The short version: a visitor has a normal conversation, the chat captures their name and email, sends them to your calendar to pick a time, and the booking shows up tagged in your CRM — start to finish, without anyone on your team lifting a finger.
